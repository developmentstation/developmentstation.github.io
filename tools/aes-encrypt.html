About AES Encryption

                    

                        

                            
Advanced Encryption Standard

                            
AES is a symmetric encryption algorithm adopted by the U.S. government and used worldwide. It's considered secure and efficient for protecting sensitive data.

                        

                        

                            
Key Sizes

                            
    
                                
  • 128-bit: Fast, suitable for most applications
    • 
                                
  • 192-bit: More secure, balanced performance
    • 
                                
  • 256-bit: Highest security, government grade
    • 
                            

                        

                        

                            
Encryption Modes

                            
    
                                
  • GCM: Authenticated encryption (recommended)
    • 
                                
  • CBC: Cipher Block Chaining
    • 
                                
  • CTR: Counter mode
    • 
                            

                        

                        

                            
Security Notes

                            
    
                                
  • All encryption is performed client-side
    • 
                                
  • Keys are never transmitted or stored
    • 
                                
  • Use strong, unique passwords
    • 
                                
  • Keep your encryption keys secure
    •
